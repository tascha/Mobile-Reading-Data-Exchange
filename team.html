Meet the Mobile Reading Data Exchange Team

Dr. Jason Young is a Senior Research Scientist at the Technology & Social Change (TASCHA) Group and the PI for the Mobile Reading Data Exchange project.

Lucas Koepke is a Data Analyst at the Technology & Social Change (TASCHA) Group and for the Mobile Reading Data Exchange project.

Erin McAweeney is a second year Master of Science in Information Management student at the University of Washington Information School and a Research Assistant for the Mobile Reading Data Exchange project.

Bree Norlander is a researcher at the University of Washington Information School and for the Mobile Reading Data Exchange project.

Dr. Jevin West is an Assistant Professor in the Information School at the University of Washington and co-directs the DataLab. He is an advisor to the Mobile Reading Data Exchange project.

Zev Lowe’s passion lies in using technology and data to create positive social change. At Worldreader, Zev turns data into insights, problem-solves, and makes organizational metrics intuitive and transparent. He also incubates new areas of business, managing relationships with partners and supporters and taking ideas to scale.

Rachel Heavner is the Global Insights Coordinator at Worldreader. She supports Worldreader’s mission by showcasing the organizations work through research, reporting, global data management and knowledge sharing. With a MA in International Educational Development from Teachers College, Columbia University, Rachel brings experience in publishing, education, marketing, operations and research to the Worldreader team.

Dror Matalon and his team develop and manage the software and technology Worldreader needs to process, maintain, and analyze the books that make up Worldreader’s collection. With over 20 years of software development and coding experience, Dror has built technology groups and teams from scratch and is a seasoned manager of teams all around the world.

Periša Ražnatović is responsible for the design and implementation of Worldreader’s Lifelong Reading programs, designed to drive educational impact among lifelong readers through Worldreader’s mobile reading platform.

Catherine Ojo works on Worldreader’s Monitoring and Evaluation team. Her background is in mathematics, control systems engineering, and neuroinformatics with underlying interest in researching, modelling, and analysing problems.. In addition to interdisciplinary research in different sectors, she has worked within the analytics and/or software development teams of information technology firms and a trading firm.

Sergio Grau is a key member of Worldreader’s technology team and brings experience in data, software and data publishing. He has more than 15 years of experience in different academic institutions such as Carnegie Mellon University (USA), Nottingham Trent University (UK), and the University of Oxford (UK). Sergio holds a Specialized Master from Telecom Bretagne (France), and an M.Sc. degree in Pattern Recognition and Artificial Intelligence from the Universidad Politécnica de Valencia.
